CASE STUDY: PHISHING ATTACK

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers pretend 
to be a trusted person or organization to steal sensitive 
information like passwords, OTPs, or bank details.

How It Happens (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: The attacker creates a fake website or email that 
looks exactly like a real bank, college portal, or 
government website.

Step 2: The victim receives an email or WhatsApp message 
saying "Your account will be blocked! Click here to verify."

Step 3: The victim clicks the link and lands on the 
fake website.

Step 4: The victim enters their login ID, password, or OTP 
thinking it is a real website.

Step 5: The attacker captures this information and uses it 
to access the victim's real account.

Who is Usually Targeted?

- College students and young adults
- People who are new to online banking or UPI
- Senior citizens who are less familiar with digital platforms
- Anyone who receives urgent-looking messages

Consequences

- Financial loss - money stolen from bank accounts
- Identity theft - personal data misused
- Loss of access to important accounts
- Emotional distress and embarrassment
